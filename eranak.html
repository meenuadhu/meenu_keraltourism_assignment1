Ernakulam





Have you ever wanted to visit the place that changed the course of history for Medieval India? Do you want to tread the land where the Europeans set foot and eventually colonized it? The place where Christianity was introduced in India? If yes, then visit Ernakulam, a district and commercial capital of Kerala. The district of Ernakulam lies in the central part of Kerela, is replete with a history that changed the course of this country.\Ernakulam is the third highest populated district, and Greater Cochin is the biggest agglomeration of the state and the primary source of its revenue. Indian Railways have three stations here Ernakulam Junction, Town, and Ernakulam Terminus. Famous for ancient churches, the city enjoys the maximum tourist footfall in the Kerela region.Situated by the Arabian Sea, Ernakulam promises some breathtaking views of the sea and sunset. As an Information Technology hub, Ernakulam is dotted with majestic buildings. Further, it houses important buildings like the High Court of Kerala and Lakshadweep, the Cochin Stock Exchange and the Port of Kochi..
